# Naledi Screenshot Plan

## Goal
Create a screenshot set that feels soft, premium, and easy to understand in under a few seconds.

## Visual direction
- Use the blush pink Naledi palette already in the app
- Keep backgrounds light and airy
- Avoid cluttered text overlays
- Use short, emotional headlines
- Show real UI, not generic placeholders

## Recommended screenshot order
### 1. Welcome / identity
- Screen: Onboarding welcome screen
- Headline: `A little light for your rhythm`
- Support line: `A softer way to track your cycle`

### 2. Today view
- Screen: Home screen with the `Today` card visible
- Headline: `See your cycle at a glance`
- Support line: `Your day, phase, and next steps in one calm space`

### 3. Quick check-in
- Screen: Home quick check-in chips or Log screen
- Headline: `Log how you feel in seconds`
- Support line: `Mood, symptoms, flow, and notes made simple`

### 4. Predictions
- Screen: Home with cycle strip, summary card, or calendar
- Headline: `Stay ahead of your rhythm`
- Support line: `See upcoming period and ovulation insights`

### 5. Insights
- Screen: Insights screen with trends and timeline
- Headline: `Notice patterns more clearly`
- Support line: `Recurring moods and symptoms, all in one place`

### 6. Personal space
- Screen: Settings / personalization
- Headline: `Make it feel like yours`
- Support line: `Private, warm, and built around your rhythm`

## Screenshot text rules
- Keep each headline under 6 words
- Keep each support line under 12 words
- Use sentence case or title case consistently
- Do not overcrowd the image with too much copy

## iPhone screenshot sizes to prepare
- 1290 x 2796
- 1242 x 2688
- 1284 x 2778

## Android screenshot sizes to prepare
- 1080 x 1920 minimum
- 1242 x 2208 or similar polished export for marketing quality

## Capture guidance
- Hide debug overlays and browser chrome
- Use consistent status bar treatment
- Keep the same visual language across all screenshots
- Prefer screens with populated data, not empty states
